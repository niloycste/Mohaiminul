Thesis

My thesis delved into the primary challenge of supply chain-based blockchain: its limited throughput, resulting in slow transaction speeds. The aim was to address this issue by introducing the concept of region-based parallel mining, grouping transactions into distinct subsets. This methodology increased the TPS rate and improved the traceability of linked transactions within the blockchain.

As the number of concurrent jobs in a chain code increased, the response time of this supply chain-based blockchain grew exponentially. Additionally, data retrieval was time-consuming; for instance, while 20 queries resulted in a 0.5-second response time, 50 inquiries took significantly longer, approximately 1 second. This low TPS rate stemmed from serial transaction processing and verification.

For a detailed exploration of my thesis, you can download the PDF document: Msc Thesis PDF
